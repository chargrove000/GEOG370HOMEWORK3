HOMEWORK.3 - World Projections Map by Claire Hargrove

Using QGIS, I created different projections by utilizing the indicatrix mapper and rendering specific projections.

WGS84 Projection

This projection is flat and as the regular circles move further from the equator, they become stretched latitudinally.

Aitoff Projection

This projection is cylindrical and as the circles stretch further from the equator, they elongate longitudinally.

Projection 3857: Pseudo Mercator

This projection is very long. The full extent of Antarctica is cut off as it proceeds further down. The circles become larger in area as they stray from the small circles at the equator.

Projection 53018: Sphere Winkel

This projection includes circles that elongate longitudinally as they stray from the equator, however they are more compact and are latitudinally straighter.

Projection 54034: Equal Area Cylindrical

This map is very elongated and as the circles go farther from the equator, they elongate latitudinally. The area is equal though, making the upper and bottom portion of the map compacted.

Projection 54027: Equidistant Conic

The projetion shows the globe from the top and is in the shape of a cone. As it strays from the North Pole, the circles compact longitudinally and stretch latitudinally.

Projection 102016: Azimuthal Equidistant

In this projection the globe is shown from the North Pole and as they go towards the South Pole, the circles stretch latitudinally and compress longitudinally. However the spacing between circles is more determined equally by area.

Projection 54017: Behrmann

This projection moves outward not from the equator but the 60 degree latitude. The equator is stretched longitudinally and near the poles the circles are stretched lattudinally.

Projection 54029: Van Der Grinten

In this projection the map is shaped into a circle and the circles are stretched towards each other. The circles are normal at the equator but stretch longitudinally near the poles.
